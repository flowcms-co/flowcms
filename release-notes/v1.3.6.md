## Fixes and improvements

- **Upgrades no longer pile up disk usage.** The self-host updater now reclaims unused images
  and build cache after *every* upgrade attempt, including failed and rolled-back ones, not only
  after a success. Previously a run of failed upgrades could leave their pulled image layers
  behind and slowly fill the disk (which, once full, made the next pull fail with a confusing
  "No such image"). Volumes (database and media) are never touched.
- **Disk pre-flight check before upgrading.** The updater now verifies there's enough free space
  before pulling new images, and if it's low it reclaims first and then refuses with a clear
  message ("only X GB free, about Y GB needed") instead of failing mid-pull.
- **Size-aware backup retention.** Pre-upgrade backups are now capped by total size as well as
  count, so a few large-media backups can't fill the disk on their own. The newest backup is
  always kept.
